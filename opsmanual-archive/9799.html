India (04/02/2002)

Indian institutions have been divided into Group “A” and Group “B”

Bachelors degrees requiring 3 or more years of full time study from the institutions listed in Group “A” are assessed as occupying Category B Level 7 of the Qualifications Table and will therefore be awarded 10 points.

Bachelors degrees requiring 3 or more years of full time study awarded in the First or Second Division or Class from the institutions listed in Group “B” are assessed as occupying Category B Level 7 of the Qualifications Table and will therefore be awarded 10 points.

Masters degrees and PhDs from institutions in Group “A” and Group “B” are assessed as occupying Category A Level 8 of the Qualifications Table and will therefore be awarded 12 points, provided the holder of the qualification also obtained their undergraduate (Bachelors) degree from an institution included in one of the two groups. If the Masters degree is not preceded by an undergraduate degree from an institution listed in one of the two groups, it is assessed as occupying Category B Level 7 of the Qualifications Table and will therefore be awarded 10 points.

Honours degrees from the listed institutions are assessed as occupying Category B Level 7 of the Qualifications Table and will therefore be awarded 10 points unless a NZQA Assessment Report assesses them as occupying Category A Level 7 of the Qualifications Table, in which case 11 points will be awarded.

Graduate and post graduate diplomas require a NZQA assessment for points allocation.
