BJ4.15 Submission of Expressions of Interest to the Pool

Immigration Act 1987 s13D

Expressions of Interest submitted in the prescribed manner may be entered into a Pool of Expressions of Interest (the Pool) if the person expressing interest:

  1. has confirmed that health and character requirements for entry to the Pool have been met because none of the people included in their Expression of Interest are people who:
    1. would not be granted a medical waiver (see A4.60); or
    2. are described in section 7(1) of the Immigration Act 1987 (see A5.20); and
  2. has claimed points for a minimum overall band score of IELTS 3 for English language ability (see BJ5.35); and
  3. has confirmed that they are aged 65 years or younger (see BJ5.25); and
  4. has claimed points for a minimum of three years of business experience (see BJ5.30); and
  5. has claimed points for a minimum of NZ$1.5 million of investment funds (see BJ5.40); and
  6. has confirmed that they legally own NZ$1 million of settlement funds in addition to the $1.5 million investment funds (see BJ5.45).

Note: People will not be granted a medical waiver if they:
~ require dialysis treatment, or
~ have active pulmonary tuberculosis; or
~ have severe haemophilia; or
~ have a physical incapacity that requires full time care.

Effective 27/07/2009
