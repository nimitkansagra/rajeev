WK2.20 Instructions on duration of Essential Skills work visas (17/11/2014)

  1. If an applicant meets the requirements for work visas set out for applications under Essential Skills work instructions (see WK2.5) then unless there are any special directions to the contrary, a work visa may be granted for the period for which employment is offered, up to a maximum of 3 years.
  2. There is no limit on how many further work visas may be granted. Further work visas may be granted for the period for which further employment is offered, up to a maximum of 3 years each time, if for each period of employment the applicant still meets the requirements for applications based on offers of employment as set out in WK2.5.
  3. Despite (a) above, if an applicant meets the requirements for work visa applications under Essential Skills work instructions (see WK2.5) based on an offer of employment which:
    1. is for an ANZSCO Skill Level 1 occupation; and
    2. offers a minimum base salary equal to at least that of the highest threshold stated in Residence from Work (Accredited Employer) instructions (currently $55,000 per annum, see RW2)

    then a one-off work visa may be granted for the period for which employment is offered, up to a maximum of 5 years.

    Notes:
    ~ Where an employee is to work more than 40 hours per week, the minimum base salary must be calculated on the basis of 40 hours’ work per week.
    ~ For the avoidance of doubt, the minimum base salary excludes employment related allowances (for example overtime, tool or uniform allowances, medical insurance, accommodation).

  4. Despite (a) above, if an applicant meets the requirements for work visa applications under Essential Skills work instructions (see WK2.5) based on an offer of employment which is for an ANZSCO Skill Level 4 or 5 occupation, a work visa may be granted up to a maximum of one year.
  5. Despite (a) above, if an applicant meets the requirements under Essential Skills work instructions at WK2.5.1, a work visa may be granted for 12 months in the first instance, and a further six months in exceptional circumstances.

Effective 17/11/2014
