Y4.50 People who must be refused entry permission: (COVID-19) (to 31/07/2022)

See also Immigration Act 2009 ss 22, 107, 108 and 109.

  1. Entry permission must be refused to any person, except a person listed in Y3.30 (a), who is not otherwise dealt with under Y4.1 and who is:
    1. the holder of a temporary entry class visa (except as provided for by Y3.30 (b))
    2. a person described under Schedule 2 of the Immigration (Visa, Entry Permission, and Related Matters) Regulations 2010 (visa-waiver travellers).
  2. A person subject to (a) above may still be granted entry permission by an immigration officer, or by the Minister of Immigration, as an exception to instructions under s 108(9) or s109(7) of the Immigration Act 2009.

Notes:
For the avoidance of doubt:
- New Zealand citizens are not subject to this instruction
- People subject to regulation 25 of the Immigration (Visa, Entry Permission, and Related Matters) Regulations 2010 are deemed to be granted entry permission
- Australians who are issued with an invitation to apply under S5.10 or S5.15 for a resident visa on arrival in New Zealand are not subject to this instruction

Effective 1/12/2021
