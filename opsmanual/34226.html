U3.1 Summary (29/11/2010)

See also Immigration Act 2009 s 4

  1. Unless otherwise specified, to be granted a student visa to attend a course of more than three months, applicants must:
    1. have an offer of a place in an approved course with an education provider in New Zealand that meets student visa requirements (see U3.5 and U5.1); and
    2. be exempted from or have paid course fees; and
    3. have a guarantee of accommodation (see U3.15); and
    4. meet the conditions set out in E3.20; and
    5. not be the holder of a current visitor visa granted under Guardians accompanying students to New Zealand instructions (see V3.100).
  2. All applicants must meet the requirements under Generic Temporary Entry Class instructions for:
    1. lodging an application for a temporary entry class visa as set out at E4; and
    2. bona fide applicants as set out at E5; and
    3. health and character as set out at A4 and A5.
  3. Applicants who have not completed, or will not be completing the course of study endorsed on their student visa and who wish to change their course of study and/or education provider must:
    1. meet requirements (a) and (b) above; and
    2. meet the requirements set out at U3.40.

      Note: Applicants who are progressing to further study, for example a student in Year 13 who is progressing to tertiary studies, will not need to meet U3.40.

Effective 29/11/2010
