** Requires evidence of a Bachelor Degree

Note: Award certificates must be provided in their original language for all qualifications.

All honorary degrees must be referred to the NZQA for an International Qualification Assessment.

If any courses leading to the award of one of the qualifications listed above were studied at any institution outside of China, (including any Chinese overseas campuses), or any courses which have been cross-credited towards the listed qualifications, please refer to the NZQA for an International Qualification Assessment.

All qualifications awarded after 2013 should be referred to NZQA for an International Qualification Assessment.

Between 1985 and 1992, the degree certificate and graduation certificate were combined into a single booklet called the “Graduation Certificate”. This booklet must state both 准予毕业 (approved for graduation) and 授予学位 (awarded and academic degree). From 1993, the degree certificate and graduation certificate were separated and students were awarded with two certificates - the degree certificate and graduation certificate.

All degrees awarded from 1985 must be endorsed with "Awarded according to the Regulations Concerning Academic Degrees in the People's Republic of China" (中华人民共和国学位条例).
