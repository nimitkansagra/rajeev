I1.20 Conditions and restrictions of interim visas (23/12/2019)

I1.20.1 Conditions of interim visas

  1. The conditions on each interim visa will depend on the type of temporary visa held by the applicant, and the type of visa applied for.

    Notes:Open work conditions are conditions that allow work in any occupation, for any employer, in any location in New Zealand.

  3. Open work visas include several types of applications: asylum seeker, working holiday maker, post-study work visa – open, partnership, partner of a worker, partner of a student, victim of family violence, mass arrival and victim of people trafficking.
  4. Open student conditions are conditions that allow study in any programme of study, at any educational institute, in any location in New Zealand.
  5. Travel conditions will not be applied to the interim visa.
  6. Any variation of conditions on the current temporary visa will be applied to the interim visa if the temporary visa applied for is of the same type as the temporary visa held, except where I1.20.1 (g) below applies.
  7. A variation of conditions held on a current temporary visa may not be applied to an interim visa granted with visitor conditions, except where a person holds a current visitor visa that was granted on the basis of guardianship of a student (see V3.100).

I1.20.5 Restrictions of interim visas

Immigration Act 2009 s 71; 79; 80

The holder of an interim visa may not apply for any visa while the interim visa is current.

Effective 23/12/2019
