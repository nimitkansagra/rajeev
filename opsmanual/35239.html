Russia (29/11/2010)

A Diplom ob okonchanii vysshego uchebnego zavedeniya – Diploma of Specialist with professional/specialist title – requiring five or more years of university level study is assessed as occupying Level 7 of the NZQF and will therefore qualify for 50 points.

A Masters Degree, Kandidat Nauk and a Doktor Nauk are assessed as occupying Level 9 or 10 of the NZQF and will therefore qualify for 55 points, provided the holder of the qualification also obtained their undergraduate (Bachelors) degree from an institution included on the List of Qualifications Exempt from Assessment. If the Kandidat Nauk or Doktor Nauk is not preceded by an undergraduate degree from an institution included on the List of Qualifications Exempt from Assessment, it is assessed as occupying Level 7 of the Register and will therefore qualify for 50 points.

The above Russian qualifications must be awarded by a State Higher Educational Institution of Russia as listed by the Russian Federation National Committee for Higher Education below. More information about these institutions may be found on their web site at: www.russianenic.ru/english/cred/index.html
